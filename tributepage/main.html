William John "Bill" Evans

Jazz pianist and composer

Here is a timeline of some important events that occurred during his life.

  • 1929 - Born in Plainfield, New Jersey
  • 1934 to 1936 - Began classical piano lessons between age 5 and 7.
  • 1936 - Started playing the violin, piccolo, and flute lessons, but dropped them shortly after.
  • 1941 - First exposed to jazz on the radio.
  • 1942 - Sat in for a sick pianist in Buddy Valentino's rehearsal band. He soon began performing for dances and weddings, playing genres like boogie woogie and polkas for a dollar per hour.
  • 1946 - attended Southeastern Louisiana University on a flute scholarship, studying classical piano interpretation with Kohnop, Venettozzi, Stetzel, and most important of all, Gretchen Magee.
  • 1948 - Composed his first piece, "Very Early".
  • 1950 - Joined Herbie Field's band, where he played piano in Chicago, Illinois.
  • 1951 to 1954 - Was drafted into the army and played flute, piccolo, and piano in the 5th U.S. Army Band at Fort Sheridan
  • 1953 - Composed his most popular song called "Waltz for Debby". Began to use recreational drugs like marijuana occasionaly.
  • 1955 - Returned to NY and enrolled in the Mannes College of Music for music composition. Joined singer Lucy Reed and her band to record an album called The Singing Reed
  • 1956 - Began to work and record with George Russell. Later released a debut album with Riverside Records called New Jazz Conceptions
  • 1958 - Joined Miles Davis' sextet, but left soon after due to his ill father. Later recorded the trio album Everybody Digs Bill Evans
  • 1959 - Rejoined Davis' sextet to record Kind of Blue
  • 1960 - Began touring in a trio with Scott LaFaro and Paul Motian.
  • 1962 - Created two new albums,Moon Beams and How My Heart Sings! with a new trio
  • 1963 - Released The Solo Sessions, Vol. 1 & 2, which were heavily affected by his heroin addiction.
  • 1966 - Met bassist Eddie Gomez, who influenced his album Bill Evans at the Montreux Jazz Festival (1968). 2 years later, drummer Marty morell joined the trio.
  • 1971 - Recorded The Bill Evans Album.
  • 1971 to 1980 - Created many new compositions and albums with different trios, including his own trio. Drug addiction became a big problem for him.
  • 1980 - Bill Evans, at age 51, died from many illnesses, but it was said it was mainly caused by drug addiction.

"My creed for art in general is that it should enrich the soul; it should teach spirituality by showing a person a portion of himself that he would not discover otherwise...a part of yourself you never knew existed." -Bill Evans
